# Taste IP System

## Core Idea

This account is not a travel account, a fashion account, or a moodboard dump.

It is a personal taste IP:

> I look at the world, filter it through a specific point of view, and turn that taste into references, language, objects, and eventually products.

The account earns trust by showing consistent judgment.

The audience follows because they want to borrow that judgment.

## North Star

- Quiet, but not empty
- Cool, but not performative
- Refined, but not luxury-flexing
- Daily, but not boring
- Strange enough to have taste
- Practical enough to become products

## What This Is

- A visual archive with a point of view
- A taste diary
- A product research lab in public
- A personal edit of fashion, objects, rooms, food, video, articles, and culture
- A future base for small products, zines, edits, objects, guides, and selected goods

## What This Is Not

- Not a generic moodboard dump
- Not a stock image account
- Not a pure fashion repost account
- Not a travel checklist account
- Not a cute lifestyle account
- Not an influencer aesthetic account
- Not a luxury-logo account
- Not a content farm

## Taste Rules

- Good things do not need to shout.
- A thing can be expensive, but it cannot flex.
- A thing can be weird, but it cannot feel cheap.
- A thing can be old, but it cannot feel sloppy.
- A thing can be minimal, but it cannot be empty.
- A thing can be daily, but it must have some tension.
- A thing can be beautiful, but it needs a reason to be here.

## Content Pillars

### 1. Daily Archive

Daily moodboard or link pack around one theme.

Examples:
- quiet uniform
- airport objects
- hotel desk
- cold spring
- anti-influencer travel
- city coffee but colder
- rooms I trust
- things that look better used

Purpose:
Build daily taste recognition.

### 2. Lookbook Notes

Fashion references, runway, brand lookbooks, styling proportions.

Focus:
- silhouette
- fabric
- color
- posture
- styling logic
- why it feels right

Purpose:
Train the audience to understand the taste behind clothes.

### 3. Moving Taste

Fashion films, campaign videos, art videos, music videos, experimental moving image.

Focus:
- pacing
- framing
- sound
- attitude
- styling
- strange energy

Purpose:
Give the account a less static, more cinematic edge.

### 4. Reading Taste

Articles, interviews, essays, cultural observations.

Focus:
- why a trend matters
- what a designer is reacting to
- what people are tired of
- how culture is shifting

Purpose:
Make the account feel intelligent, not just pretty.

### 5. Product Seeds

Anything that could become a product later.

Examples:
- tote
- notebook
- zine
- travel pouch
- desk object
- coffee object
- city guide
- digital moodboard pack
- selected goods list
- small clothing capsule

Purpose:
Turn taste into commercial direction.

## Daily Workflow

1. Gather 15-30 links.
2. Sort them into lookbook, video, article, object, space, or product seed.
3. Pick one daily topic.
4. Write one taste judgment.
5. Build one post around that judgment.
6. Record user feedback.
7. Update taste memory.
8. Save promising product seeds.

## Daily Output Format

Each daily pack should include:

- Date
- Daily topic
- Why this topic today
- 6-10 lookbook / image links
- 4-8 video links
- 5-8 article links
- 1 recommended Xiaohongshu post
- 2-3 title options
- Caption draft
- Product seed notes
- Feedback prompt

## Xiaohongshu Post Structure

### Title

A taste judgment, not just a description.

Good:
- 最近越来越喜欢“不主动讨好”的东西
- 旅行穿搭别太用力，像这样就够了
- 我想把小红书做成一本私藏视觉档案
- 9张图：冷一点的春天

Weak:
- 今日 moodboard
- 好看的图片分享
- 最近喜欢的风格
- 一些穿搭参考

### Body

Recommended rhythm:

1. State the mood.
2. Name the visual ingredients.
3. Say what it rejects.
4. Say why it matters.
5. End lightly.

Template:

```text
最近想存一组 [topic]。

不是 [what it rejects]。
更像是 [visual ingredients / feeling]。

[One short cultural or personal judgment.]

如果你也喜欢这种 [taste phrase]，可以先存。
```

## Voice

The voice should feel:

- calm
- concise
- slightly private
- observant
- not begging for attention
- not over-explaining
- not too marketing-like

Avoid:

- "姐妹们冲"
- "太绝了"
- "高级感拉满"
- "氛围感天花板"
- "普通人必看"
- "谁懂啊"
- "狠狠拿捏"

Prefer:

- "最近越来越喜欢..."
- "这组不是在讲..."
- "它吸引我的地方是..."
- "有些东西不需要太明确地表达自己。"
- "好东西有时候只是比例对了。"

## Feedback Labels

Use these labels to correct the system:

- 对味
- 不对味
- 太普通
- 太网红
- 太甜
- 太商业
- 更冷
- 更狂
- 更日常
- 更文化
- 更产品
- 适合发
- 只适合参考
- 有产品潜力
- 可以做栏目
- 像我
- 不像我

## Product Direction

The product path should start small.

Phase 1:
- digital moodboard pack
- PDF visual notes
- zine
- curated link list
- city object guide
- travel taste guide

Phase 2:
- notebook
- tote
- pouch
- postcard set
- desk object
- coffee object
- selected goods list

Phase 3:
- small capsule collection
- collaborative product
- pop-up
- paid community / newsletter

## Weekly Review

Every week, review:

- Which topics felt most "me"?
- Which links got saved?
- Which posts could become a series?
- Which repeated objects appeared?
- Which themes have product potential?
- Which parts felt too generic?

Weekly output:

- 3 strongest topics
- 3 rejected directions
- 3 product seeds
- 1 post format to repeat
- 1 adjustment to taste memory

## Current Operating Principle

Moodboard is the surface.

Taste is the asset.

Product is the long game.
